Open with the brief: what was the problem or invitation? What constraints shaped the work — a budget, a deadline, a particular audience, an existing brand? A short paragraph here gives a reader something to anchor the visuals to.

Continue with process: what did you try, what did you throw out, what surprised you? Specifics earn trust. A sentence about a material, a reference, or a happy accident is worth more than a paragraph of vague descriptors.

Close with outcome or reflection: where did it land, what did it teach you, what would you do differently. If there were collaborators, credit them here.
